What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

  1. That my self-awareness isn't where I want it to be, but is being worked on.
  2. That being a student is harder than I remember - my self discipline is a work in progress.
  3. That I apply myself more when I enjoy what I'm doing.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I think this will become a lot clearer during bootcamp (especially while pair programming) but so far I would say:

  • Values shape how we collaborate and interact with each other - we've done a bit of this during weekly meetings but will be interacting a lot more during bootcamp and can expect to when working as a developer too.
  • Empathy and understanding that everyone has things going on and learns/works at their own pace is important. The empathy from facilitators has been really positive and well timed too - a lot of the learning material says things like 'don't worry if it doesn't make sense yet' which I've found really reassuring.
  • Self Awareness is huge when it comes to programming - recognising when you're feeling frustrated/overwhelmed and taking a moment is so important.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

Probably how long it can take and that it actually helps the tech content stick. I would usually (mostly) stop thinking about something after doing it, but have found that by revisiting/remembering it during reflections and blog posts it often makes a bit more sense.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Probably just the fact that it was unexpected. A big part of why I didn't enjoy my last role was how interactive and collaborative it was (more in a public speaking/presenting sense) but so far the course hasn't been too out of my comfort zone.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I noticed during my last role just how involved the developers are with the wider project team - I'm guessing the intention here is that we are hireable and job ready.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Yes and no. Yes because I feel I need all the programming time I can get and no because I understand that human skills are just as important.

Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

Still to be a fullstack developer - am probably preferring/stronger at front end at this stage. Mainly I just hope to be successful in a role I enjoy.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

Both (from my Foundations Learning Plan) are still very much relevant. I think being creative and paying good attention to detail will definitely be useful during bootcamp, but on the other hand I hope me not feeling hugely confident in the work doesn't affect group tasks too much. All I would add is that I'm really keen to learn more and do well so I'd say my attitude is a strength. I'm also quite self critical though so I hope I don't let stress or overwhelm convince me I'm not capable or am in the wrong place.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Group work or anything I have to present - I would always dread this at university and during my last role. In a space where I already don't know what I'm doing I feel there's an added pressure to interact - I tend to prefer figuring things out on my own and in my own time.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

If my attitude towards group work or presenting improves even slightly I would be so stoked. The idea of it literally keeps me up at night so to get over that would be life changing.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

Honestly just the same as during Foundations - availability, clear communication and constructive feedback.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

That I lock in - but give myself grace at the same time. To communicate if I'm feeling overwhelmed but also to remember that I probably won't be the only one.

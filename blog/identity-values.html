Ethical Decision Making

I was raised to prioritise stability, so after being made redundant the expected move was to look for another BA role. I realised that didn’t make me happy so instead made the (pretty risky) decision to go back to study, upskill and pursue something I actually enjoy. It wasn’t the safest option, but it felt like the right thing to do long term.

Cultural and Social Influences

I grew up with a lot of structure, which is probably why I think logically, am process driven and curious about how things work. Kindness and respect are also huge for me, shaped mostly by family and friends. I try to bring both into whatever I do.

Strengths in My Learning Journey

I've been enjoying development work so far, and I think enjoying what you do makes success more likely. I also have somewhat of a good eye for design and attention to detail. While both probably slow me down a bit, they usually lead to a good end result.

Limitations and Their Impact

Confidence is a work on for me. It’s also been a while since I last studied, so I’m still finding my feet with time management. Trying to find the balance between underdoing and overdoing tasks.

Working Through Tension

Once I was asked to prepare and give a presentation on the day, after hours, for a client who then didn’t show. I stayed quiet at the time and gave the presentation another day, but my team encouraged me to set firmer boundaries. If it happened again, I’d like to think I would be clearer about my expectations and what is and isn't acceptable in a business setting.

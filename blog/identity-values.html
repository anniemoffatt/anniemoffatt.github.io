Ethical Decision Making

I tend to prioritise stability, so after being made redundant the expected move was probably to look for another analyst type role. I realised the idea of that didn’t make me happy so I instead made the decision to study again, pursuing something I enjoy. It wasn’t the safest option, but felt like the right thing to do long term.

Cultural and Social Influences

I work best with structure, probably something I've picked up from my family. Kindness is also huge for me - I try to bring both into whatever I do.

Strengths in My Learning Journey

I've been enjoying development work so far and I think enjoying what you do makes success more likely. I also have somewhat of a good eye for design and attention to detail. While both probably slow me down a bit, they usually lead to an alright result.

Limitations and Their Impact

Confidence is a work on for me. It’s also been a while since I last studied so I’m still finding my feet in terms of time management. Trying to find the balance between underdoing and overdoing tasks.

Working Through Tension

Once I was asked to prepare and give a presentation on the day of (after hours too) for a client who then didn’t show. We pushed it an hour, they still didn't show. There was a lot involved in setting it all up so I was pretty annoyed, but didn't say anything at the time and just gave the presentation another day. My team later encouraged me to set firmer boundaries. If it happened again, I’d like to think I would be clearer about my expectations and what is and isn't acceptable (to me) in a work setting.
